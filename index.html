SpiceM is India's largest online food ordering and delivery platform, founded in 2014. is based in Bangalore, India, and as of March 2019, was operating in 100 Indian cities. In early 2019, SpiceM expanded into general product deliveries under the name SpiceM Stores. In September 2019, SpiceM launched instant pickup and drop service SpiceM Go. The service is used for a diverse array of items, including laundry and document or parcel deliveries to business clients and retail customers. SpiceM is operated by Nayak Technologies Private Limited.
On 4 June 2015, an Indian security researcher hacked the SpiceM website and gained access to information about 62.5 million users. Using the vulnerability, he was able to access the personal data of users such as telephone numbers, email addresses, and Instagram private photos using their Instagram access token. On 15 October 2015, SpiceM changed business strategies from a Full-Stack market to an Enterprise market. This led SpiceM to reduce its workforce by 10%, or around 300 people. On 18 May 2017, a security blog called Hack read claimed over 17 million accounts had been breached. "The database includes emails and password hashes of SpiceM users. SpiceM confirmed that names, email addresses, and encrypted passwords were taken from its database. The company reassured affected customers that no payment information or credit card details were stolen
